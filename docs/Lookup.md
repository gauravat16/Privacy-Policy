Last updated: April 6, 2026

Lookup is a dictionary and vocabulary app. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app may process and how it is used.

## What Lookup processes

Depending on the features you use, Lookup may process:

- text you choose to look up, share, or send through Android text selection;
- clipboard text, if you enable optional clipboard lookup on supported Android versions;
- lookup history, saved words, notes, and app settings stored on your device;
- backup files you choose to export, import, or sync with Google Drive;
- notification data such as a Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token;
- limited device and app information needed for app features, notifications, ads, or backup services;
- advertising identifiers or similar identifiers used by Google AdMob in the free version.

Lookup does not require an in-app account.

## How the data is used

Lookup uses data to:

- show definitions and related content;
- support lookup from shared text, selected text, or clipboard text when enabled;
- save your history, notes, favorites, and preferences locally on your device;
- import, export, and sync backups when you choose to use those features;
- send notifications;
- display ads in supported free versions of the app;
- maintain and improve app functionality.

## Local storage

Most user data is stored locally on your device, including history, notes, saved words, cached content, and app settings.

You can remove local data by clearing app storage, uninstalling the app, or deleting backup files you created.

## Clipboard, sharing, and selected text

Lookup can process text you intentionally send to it through Android sharing or text-selection features.

If you enable clipboard lookup, the app reads copied text on your device to show a lookup popup. This feature is optional and is not required to use the app.

## Backup and Google Drive

If you use backup features, Lookup may create or restore backup files containing your app data, such as history, notes, saved words, cached content, and settings.

If you enable Google Drive backup, the app uses your Google account and Google Drive app-scoped access to upload, download, update, or delete your backup file. Google may process this data under its own privacy terms.

## Ads and notifications

The free version of Lookup may use Google AdMob to show banner or interstitial ads. AdMob and its partners may process data such as advertising identifiers, device information, and app interaction data under their own policies.

Lookup also uses Firebase Cloud Messaging to send notifications, which may involve processing your device's FCM token and related messaging data.

## Third-party services

When you use online dictionary, translation, or web-based definition features, the word or text you request may be sent to the relevant third-party service to return results.

These services may include Google, Google Drive, Firebase, Google AdMob, Urban Dictionary, Wikipedia, Cambridge Dictionary, Merriam-Webster, Collins, Dictionary.com, Thesaurus.com, Vocabulary.com, Oxford, Yandex translation services, and Livio dictionary integrations.

Your use of those services is also subject to their own privacy policies.

## Data sharing

We do not sell your personal information.

Lookup may share data only as needed to provide app features, including with Google AdMob, Firebase Cloud Messaging, Google Drive, and the third-party content services you choose to use through the app.

## Permissions used by the app

- `POST_NOTIFICATIONS`: used to show word reminders, backup progress, review prompts, and other app notifications.
- `VIBRATE`: used to provide vibration feedback and notification vibration where supported.
- `RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED`: used to restore enabled background features or scheduled notifications after device restart.
- `WAKE_LOCK`: used to help complete background tasks such as notifications or service work reliably.
- `INTERNET`: used to fetch online definitions, translation results, ads, notifications, and Google Drive backup data.
- `ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE`: used to check whether the device is online before using network-based features.
- `FOREGROUND_SERVICE`: used to run user-visible background features such as the optional clipboard monitoring service.
- `WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE`: used on supported Android versions to export backup files to user-selected storage.
- `READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE`: used on supported Android versions to import backup files chosen by the user.
- `com.ichi2.anki.permission.READ_WRITE_DATABASE`: used when the user chooses to integrate Lookup with AnkiDroid flashcards.
- `com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID`: used by Google AdMob in supported builds to serve and measure ads.
- `FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE`: used for the app's special-use foreground clipboard service on supported Android versions.

## Your choices

You can limit data processing by:

- disabling optional clipboard lookup;
- disabling notifications;
- not using online lookup, translation, ads-supported builds, or Google Drive backup features;
- deleting local data or backup files you created.

## Children's privacy

Lookup is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.
